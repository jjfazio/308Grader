1.5.5. PowerTeacher

PowerTeacher is a multi-platform classroom management system created by Pearson. It works with PowerSchool: a web-based student information system, to connect with ten million students in all fifty states and sixty-five countries. PowerSchool can be an advantage or disadvantage depending on external variables (funding, district, etc...). Because PowerTeacher supports multiple platforms, it leaves teachers/professors untethered from their desktop and laptop and allows them to record scores and make observations while moving around the classroom, gym, playground, lab or even field trips.

Good Features:

  • Create, update, and manage assignments
  • View and update class and group metrics (mean, median, mode) • Create, update, and manage assignment categories
  • Integrated help
  • Send email to student contacts
  • Past assignments and scores to parents and students in real-time • Record scores for assignments
  • View and update class and group metrics (mean, median, mode) • Record or auto-calculates student final grade
  • Mass fill scores for assignments
  • Enter comments and score codes for assignments
  • Review class rosters
  • Access student demographics
  • Access student alerts
  • Send email to student contacts
  • View student photos from the office
  • View scoresheet for student groups
  • Create, update, and manage assignment categories
  • Review grade scales
  • Reporting terms to filter assignments and final grades
  • Integrated help
  • Secure login
  • School Locator
  • Synchronize with the PowerTeacher web grade book
  • Virtual Chalkboard

Bad Features:

  • Dependency on PowerSchool
  • Some features are are available or absent on different platforms
  • Abundance of features that one operational setting may or may not use

Missing Features:

  • Student can view other grades (names omitted)
  • Online turn in with auto grading
  • Abundance of features that one operational setting may or may not use
  • View what is needed to get desired grade
